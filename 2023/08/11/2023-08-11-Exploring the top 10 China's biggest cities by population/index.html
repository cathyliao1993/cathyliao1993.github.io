2023-08-11

Top 10 Largest Cities in China by Population()

China is home to over 600 cities, with a total population exceeding 1.4 billion. About 260 million people reside in just 15 of these cities. To classify city size, the number of permanent residents is often used as a key criterion. China has 19 “supercities” (each with over 5 million people) and 3 “megacities” (each with over 20 million residents). Some of the most populous cities include Chongqing, Shanghai, Beijing, Chengdu, Tianjin, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Wuhan, Harbin, and Suzhou.

1. Chongqing – 31.2 Million People

Chongqing is the largest city in China by population due to its favorable geographical location, serving as a key transportation hub that connects different regions. As a major industrial and commercial center in western China, Chongqing has benefited from long-standing policies encouraging growth, drawing in migrants from across the country. Its role as a shelter city during the Sino-Japanese War further solidified its large population base.

2. Shanghai – 24.3 Million People

Shanghai, one of the world’s busiest ports, stands as China’s second-largest city and its economic powerhouse. Located in the Yangtze River Delta, it has attracted international corporations for centuries. Shanghai’s development into a global metropolis has been fueled by its financial markets, IT sector, and strategic transport infrastructure, including Pudong and Hongqiao airports.

3. Beijing – 21.5 Million People

Beijing, China’s capital, is often mistaken for the largest city due to its historical and political importance. As the third-largest by population, Beijing is a cultural and economic hub, boasting a rich history that spans over 3,000 years. Its advanced transport networks and role as a center for finance and technology make it a magnet for both locals and expatriates.

4. Chengdu – 16.6 Million People

Known as the “Land of Abundance,” Chengdu’s favorable climate and geography have made it an important agricultural and commercial center since ancient times. Today, it’s a growing metropolis in southwestern China, attracting talent and business from around the globe. Its relaxed pace of life, famous Sichuan cuisine, and popular tourist attractions make Chengdu a desirable place to live.

5. Tianjin – 15.6 Million People

Tianjin, China’s largest port city in the north, has been a critical trade hub since the Tang Dynasty. As a key city in the Bohai Economic Rim, Tianjin’s prosperity is linked to its extensive transport infrastructure and proximity to Beijing. Its role in handling international imports and exports continues to drive its economic expansion.

6. Guangzhou – 15.3 Million People

Located in the Pearl River Delta, Guangzhou is a key city in southern China, closely connected to Hong Kong and Macau. The city’s port and Baiyun International Airport make it a gateway for international trade. Guangzhou also hosts China’s largest import/export fair, which continues to attract global corporations and young talent to the city.

7. Shenzhen – 13.4 Million People

Once a small fishing village, Shenzhen transformed into a global tech hub after being designated a special economic zone in 1980. Its rapid development in industries such as technology, finance, and trade has made it one of China’s wealthiest cities, with the highest GDP per capita among the top 10 cities.

8. Wuhan – 11.2 Million People

Wuhan’s strategic location at the confluence of the Yangtze and Han rivers has made it a key transportation and industrial hub. Known for its rich history and culture, Wuhan’s economy thrives on industries such as steel, shipbuilding, and automotive manufacturing, supported by its extensive railway network.

9. Harbin – 10.8 Million People

Harbin, in northeastern China, is known for its fertile lands and historical significance. During the 19th century, large numbers of Chinese relocated here, fleeing the Yellow River floods. Today, Harbin attracts migrants for its lower cost of living and opportunities in cross-border trade with Russia and Europe.

10. Suzhou – 10.75 Million People

Suzhou, located near Shanghai, benefits significantly from its proximity to China’s financial capital. The city’s historical focus on agriculture has shifted to industries such as IT, biotech, and artificial intelligence, making it a modern center for technological innovation.
